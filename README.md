INTRODUCTION :
Definition and importance of digital health platforms
Rise in usage due to lifestyle diseases and COVID-19
Purpose of the study: Evaluate the effectiveness of digital health tools in supporting wellness
INDUSTRY PROFILE :
The healthcare sector is a vital industry focused on improving and maintaining individual and community well-being. It includes hospitals, clinics, pharmaceuticals, insurance, and digital health services. With rising lifestyle diseases and aging populations, there's a growing demand for accessible and affordable care. 
The integration of technology like telehealth, wearable devices, and health apps is transforming the sector, making healthcare more efficient, personalized, and preventive in nature.
METHODOLOGY :
Design: Quantitative, survey-based using structured questionnaire
Sample Size: 219 respondents
Sampling Technique: Simple Random sampling
Data Collection: Google Forms distributed via social media
OBJECTIVES , TOOLS & POPULATIONS :
Primary Objective: To study the impact of digital health platforms on wellness Supports
Secondary Objectives:
Analyze perceived impact on physical, mental, emotional wellness
Assess user engagement and satisfaction
Identify demographic influences
Tools: Structured questionnaires, Likert scale, ANOVA, Regression
Population: Students, professionals, healthcare workers using digital health tools.
ANALYSIS & FINDINGS :
Descriptive Stats: Moderate satisfaction (avg. 3.35/5)
Significant Findings
ANOVA showed digital platform usage positively impacts wellness (p < 0.001)
Regression indicated engagement strongly predicts wellness outcomes
Differences found across age and occupation, not gender
Personalization features linked to user satisfaction
CONCLUSION :
Digital health platforms effectively support user wellness
Engagement and personalization are key to positive outcomes
Need for improved platform design, personalization, and accessibility
Future research should explore longitudinal effects and diverse populations
